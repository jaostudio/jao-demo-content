# Homepage Narrative Blueprint

## The Emotional Journey

The homepage is a **perception engine**. Every section converts the visitor from one emotional state to the next.

```
Section            Emotion Triggered        Question Answered
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hero               "Impressed"              Is this developer different?
Social Proof       "Safe"                   Are they credible?
Featured Work      "Capable"                Can they deliver?
Interactive        "Sophisticated"          Are they technically strong?
Services           "Relevant"               Do they do what I need?
Process            "Reliable"               Are they organized?
Tech Credibility   "Expert"                 Do they know their craft?
About              "Trustworthy"            Who am I working with?
FAQ                "Informed"              What's the risk/convenience?
CTA                "Ready to act"           Should I contact them?
Contact            "Committed"             I'm reaching out.
```

## Section-by-Section Emotional Targets

### Hero → IMPRESSED
- 5-second judgment zone
- Must feel visually premium, technically sophisticated, professionally positioned
- The right-side visual is the "holy shit" moment
- Copy must sound like a business partner, not a coder

### Social Proof → SAFE
- Rapid credibility anchors
- Lighthouse, SEO, responsive, production-ready — these are trust signals
- No fake numbers. Use real capability badges.

### Featured Projects → CAPABLE
- Proof of execution quality
- Business-oriented descriptions (outcomes, not technologies)
- Asymmetric layout signals design sophistication
- Interactive mockups signal technical depth

### Interactive Showcase → SOPHISTICATED
- "This developer can build advanced UI"
- Frontend capability as a product feature
- Select, high-quality, experimental-feeling

### Services → RELEVANT
- "They do exactly what I need"
- Match client mental categories: landing pages, dashboards, redesigns
- Short, outcome-focused service cards

### Process → RELIABLE
- "They have a system — they won't waste my time or money"
- Visual timeline builds trust through transparency
- This section converts skeptical clients

### Tech Credibility → EXPERT
- "They know modern architecture"
- Performance metrics, SEO thinking, responsive strategy
- Non-technical clients still subconsciously register professionalism

### About → TRUSTWORTHY
- "This person is professional and easy to work with"
- Short, confident, business-aware
- Stats (projects, techs, availability, location) provide concrete anchors

### FAQ → INFORMED
- Remove remaining objections
- Timeline, international work, deployment, redesign
- Answer the questions clients won't ask directly

### CTA → READY TO ACT
- "Contacting them feels like the right move"
- Cinematic scale makes the ask feel important
- Primary + secondary buttons reduce friction

### Contact → COMMITTED
- Structured form filters serious inquiries
- "Budget range" and "project type" fields position you as selective
- Calendly link for immediate action

## Narrative Rule

```
Every section should make the NEXT section feel natural,
and the PREVIOUS section feel validated.
```

If the hero says "premium developer" but the next section shows a generic skill bar, the narrative breaks.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not jump from "premium hero" to "generic skills grid"
- Do not break the emotional flow with walls of text
- Do not interrupt with low-quality elements (clipart, stock icons, random gradients)
- Do not ask for conversion before building trust (contact form at bottom only)

## The One-Second Test

A visitor should be able to scroll through the entire homepage and absorb the narrative in under 30 seconds. Each section communicates its message visually before the visitor reads a single word.
